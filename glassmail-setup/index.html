GlassMail Setup

Connect your Gmail to GlassMail with a Google App Password — a secure, revocable key you create inside your own Google account.

Why an App Password instead of "Sign in with Google"?

Apps that read Gmail must pass Google's full OAuth verification — a lengthy security review. While we complete that process, GlassMail uses a Google App Password: a feature built into your Google account that lets you grant one specific app access to your mail without ever sharing your main password. You stay in control — you can revoke GlassMail's access at any time by deleting the App Password, and it can't be used to change your account or your password.

How we protect your email

  • We never read your email. Your messages are fetched only to display them on your glasses — no one at CPR Studios reviews them.
  • Your App Password is encrypted at rest on our servers, and is used only to fetch and send your mail on your behalf.
  • We don't store your emails on our servers, and we never sell your data or use it for advertising or AI training.
  • AI replies are on-demand. Only the single email you choose to reply to is sent to our AI provider, and only when you tap to generate a draft.
  • You're in control. Revoke access anytime by deleting the GlassMail App Password in your Google account.

Create your App Password

It takes about a minute. You'll need 2-Step Verification turned on — Google requires it before it will let you create an App Password.

Go to your Google Account

Open myaccount.google.com in your browser and sign in if prompted.

Open "Security & sign-in"

Tap Security & sign-in.

Open "2-Step Verification"

Under "How you sign in to Google," tap 2-Step Verification.

Turn on 2-Step Verification

If it isn't already on, enable it and follow Google's prompts. If it's already on, just continue.

Find "App passwords"

Scroll to the bottom of the 2-Step Verification page and tap App passwords.

Name it "GlassMail" and tap Create

Type GlassMail in the "App name" field, then tap Create.

Copy the App Password

Google shows a 16-character password. Copy it now — you won't be able to see it again.

Enter it in GlassMail

Back in GlassMail, enter your email address and paste the App Password, then tap Connect.

Tip: you can paste the password with or without spaces — they're only there for readability.
